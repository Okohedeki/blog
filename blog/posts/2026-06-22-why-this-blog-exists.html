Why this blog exists

I build a lot of things at once, and most of them never get explained anywhere. The code is on GitHub, the reasoning is in my head, and the gap between the two is where projects go to be forgotten. This is the place I'm closing that gap.

It's a working log, not a magazine. Posts here will be short and specific: a decision I made and why, something that broke, a thing I changed my mind about. If you want the polished version, read the README. If you want to know what I was actually thinking, read this.

The throughline

If there's one thing the projects have in common, it's that they're built to keep working without me. VaultOP runs entirely on-device, so nothing leaves the machine. Airlock puts you inside the agent's loop so it can't run off without supervision. markov-engine grows a knowledge base on its own after you hand it a single link.

I'm less interested in tools you operate and more in tools that hold a commitment for you.

That's the lens I'll be writing through. Expect notes on local-first design, agent runtimes that you can actually control, and the small engineering decisions that make systems trustworthy enough to leave alone.

What to expect

  • Build logs from whatever's most active that week
  • Short post-mortems when something breaks in an interesting way
  • The occasional opinion I'm willing to defend

That's it. Onward.
